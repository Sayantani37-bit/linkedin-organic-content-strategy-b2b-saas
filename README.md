# linkedin-organic-content-strategy-b2b-saas

Objective:
To examine the strategies of prominent B2B SaaS creators on LinkedIn and determine a series of replicable LinkedIn growth strategies to develop into a practical LinkedIn organic content playbook.

---

Scope

This research will look into how top B2B SaaS creators develop authority, engagement, and demand through LinkedIn, along with their respective approaches to long-form content development.

Research sources:

* LinkedIn Posts
* YouTube Interviews
* Podcasts
* Newsletters
* Published Content Frameworks

Research Goal

The end result is to identify common content patterns, growth frameworks, positioning strategies, distribution systems, and engagement tactics, as well as best practices used by top LinkedIn experts, which can then be synthesized into a practical LinkedIn organic content strategy playbook for B2B SaaS.




---

Research Process

Day 1 - LinkedIn Post Collection

* gathered recent LinkedIn posts from the examined experts.
* summarized main topics and common themes
* observed content, positioning, and community-building methods.

Day 2 - Long-form content examination

* downloaded and set up Claude Code.
* Experimented with an API-based content compilation workflow.
* extracted YouTube transcripts via the Supadata API.
* structured YouTube transcript collection process within the repository.
* started extracting the most influential frameworks from interviews/podcasts.

Research Scope

As there was no specific indication given in the task on the number of posts/videos for each individual expert. I therefore decided to use the following to provide a mix of short-form and long-form content for all experts while also having enough information for a thorough analysis:

* 5 most recent posts per expert from LinkedIn
* 1 YouTube video interview/podcast/presentation per expert

This helped to identify the common trends, framework, content strategy, positioning, and audience engagement strategy of each of the 10 experts. The emphasis was on content of relevance and quality of information rather than the quantity.



Content collected for each expert:

* 5 latest LinkedIn posts
* 1 YouTube interview/podcast/presentation transcript
* 1 YouTube transcript analysis + key learnings
* 1 content framework or insight explanation

Total research collected-

Preliminary Conclusions

Recurring themes between experts:

* Distinctive positioning gains attention
* Originality, rather than volume, matters most
* Sustainable growth comes from community
* Thought leadership compounds over time
* Content distribution is key, as important as creation.
* AI improves output but demands higher levels of originality.
