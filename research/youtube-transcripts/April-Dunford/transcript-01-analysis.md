April Dunford Podcast Summary

Main Focus:

* Product Positioning
* Category Design
* Messaging vs. Positioning
* B2B SaaS go-to-market strategies
* Personal Branding and Expertise



Key Learnings:

* Positioning determines a product's differentiation for a given customer segment.
* The failure of many great products may be attributed to placing them within an irrelevant market.
* Positioning should be driven by customer value, not business assumptions.
* The most effective startups achieve product/market fit and succeed within a narrow market before branching out. (Big Fish Small Pond).
* Personal brands are most easily constructed around an extreme niche and maintained through consistent effort.

Frameworks:


Positioning Definition Framework-

     Product
       ↓
    Differentiated Value
       ↓
    Target Customer
       ↓
    Market Category

Positioning:

Three Positioning Approaches

Big Fish, Small Pond
* Find and win a narrow market.
* Become the undisputed leader of the category.
Head-to-Head
* Direct competition with existing market leaders.
* Leverage their vulnerabilities.
Category Creation
* Creating an entirely new category (for truly innovative products only).
Positioning Review Framework

Every six months:
* Analyze your competitors
* analyze your products features and benefits
* Analyze customer value
* Update your positioning if necessary

Relevance to Organic Content on LinkedIn

Content:
* Stick to your area of expertise.
* Offer insights, not promotions.
* Educate, educate, educate.

Positioning:
* Focus your content around a niche.
* Position your expertise clearly to distinguish it from others.
* Develop authority around one specific topic.

Growth:
* Develop credibility through consistency and specialization.
* Become a recognized expert in one area of importance.

Distribution:
* Consistently reinforce your positioning.
* Ensure every piece of content is aligned to your message.

Key Learnings Summary:

* Positioning forms the basis of all messaging.
* You can effectively position your B2B SaaS company with the Big Fish Small     Pond strategy before expanding.
* Category creation should be the exception, not the rule.
* The principles for product positioning apply equally well to personal branding.
* Building authority takes specialization and sustained effort over a long period of time.
