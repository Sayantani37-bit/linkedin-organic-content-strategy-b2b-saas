Post 1

Date: June 12, 2026

Topic: How to measure B2B community impact

Summary: Dave shares that the most important factors that indicate success in B2B communities are member engagement, referrals, and direct recommendations, rather than traditional subscriber count or member attendance.

Takeaway: Strong communities generate influential advocacy and personal relationships that can't always be captured in numbers.

LinkedIn Content Insight: User-generated success stories in a B2B community add credibility and demonstrate value to prospects.

Post 2

Date: June 11, 2026

Topic: B2B marketer's perspective on AI

Summary: A survey was taken by over 500 B2B marketers on how they use AI in their work, where it was found that they are adopting it, feel cautiously optimistic about the technology, but most concerns about its impact revolve around the quality of generated content and not job loss.

Takeaway: While marketers are adopting AI, the challenge lies in not flooding the internet with AI-generated fluff and still maintaining a clear brand voice.

LinkedIn Content Insight: Survey data gives professionals insights and a better grasp of current trends; this creates value.

Post 3

Date: June 7, 2026

Topic: B2B marketing with ABM best practices

Summary: Dave explains lessons from an interview he had with the director of ABM at Snowflake on best practices that their company applies to marketing. Some of these practices include getting marketing and sales in line with one another, focusing on accounts with high potential, and the use of AI to facilitate targeted ABM approaches.

Takeaway: The success of B2B ABM comes from sales and marketing's alignment; it’s not about mass campaigns.

LinkedIn Content Insight: Educational content like expert interview snippets is highly valuable.

Post 4

Date: June 7, 2026

Topic: Why B2B communities for marketing leaders succeed

Summary: Dave breaks down the successful nature of Exit Five's CMO community. CMOs, because of the nature of their role, can feel isolated, as many feel that marketing leaders have a limited community for those at their level to get advice, network, and openly share challenges.

Takeaway: A great niche community fills an important role by solving unique professional problems and by creating belonging.

LinkedIn Content Insight: Demonstrating how a service or product addresses an audience's pain points builds trust.

Post 5

Date: June 7, 2026

Topic: A balanced approach to use AI

Summary: Dave gives us his thoughts on how readily available information through AI can diminish human curiosity, thoughtful thinking, and creativity. The importance of habit formation, such as journaling and handwritten note-taking, helps to prompt thought and idea generation.

Takeaway: Using AI and human interaction are both beneficial, as they allow for more time to develop creative thought and thinking and the time to analyze and generate further ideas from the output.

LinkedIn Content Insight: Being transparent with and humanizing thought leadership helps to foster audience connection and make content more relatable.
