Subject:
The Feature Trap vs Product-Market Fit

Briefing:
Anthony discusses the common startup mistake of continuously building new features without understanding their customers or distribution needs. He also states that certain innovative features are necessary in establishing a competitive edge and acquiring market share for the product.

Key Learning:
Not all features should be done away with. Some revolutionary features are actually the main reason for product adoption or for having an impact on growth.

Subject:
Who Should Your Homepage be Written for?

Briefing:
Anthony explains why most B2B SaaS products have their website copy aimed at business leaders and not at product champions. He explains that users who evaluate products are in need of direct answers as to what the product is and how it differs from those in the market.

Key Learning:
Homepage copy should be focused on aiding the evaluator in understanding the product rather than in stating vague business outcomes.

LinkedIn Content Insight:

Subject:
The Problem with Outcome-Based Marketing

Briefing:
Anthony believes marketers claim inflated business outcomes such as profits or customer expansion for their products. He points out that these factors are determined by several variables besides a single product or service and suggests marketers focus instead on stating truthful and measurable product advantages.

Key Learning:
B2B SaaS marketing should prioritize functional value and concrete benefits over exaggerated business promises.

LinkedIn Content Insight:
Educational articles in a longer format can boost credibility by going into more detail regarding industry issues.

Subject:
What Is Your Product?

Briefing:
Anthony identifies a key error in product positioning where products only address business outcomes or visions and never explain what the product actually does. Prospects must understand the product category and the scenario for using it before being made aware of the benefits.

Key Learning:
A strong product positioning is based on a simple clarification as to what a product is and for whom.

LinkedIn Content Insight:
The visual charts and simple diagnosis questions presented help make positioning challenges easier to understand.

Subject:
Three core positioning strategies

Briefing:
Anthony proposes 3 product positioning strategies, which are competing within an existing market category, taking an existing category to a new segment, and launching into an entirely new category. Each positioning strategy also comes with its unique benefits and pitfalls.

Key Learning:
Market category strategy must be chosen based on the company’s resources, competition, and growth objectives if the product is to be positioned correctly.

LinkedIn Content Insight:
 Framework-based content offers more value by simplifying strategic decision-making into discrete options.
